Breathwork Techniques

Breathwork is a powerful tool for mental health and overall wellness, helping to regulate the nervous system and reduce stress. By focusing on intentional breathing patterns, individuals can enhance emotional balance, improve concentration, and cultivate a sense of inner calm. Whether used for relaxation, energy renewal, or mindfulness, breathwork supports holistic well-being and strengthens the mind-body connection.

Box Breathing (Square Breathing)

Reduces stress, enhances focus, and promotes emotional regulation. It's often used by athletes and military personnel to stay calm under pressure.

  • Inhale deeply through your nose for 4 seconds.
  • Hold your breath for 4 seconds.
  • Exhale slowly through your mouth for 4 seconds.
  • Pause for 4 seconds before repeating the cycle.
  • Continue for 5-20 minutes.

Diaphragmatic Breathing (Belly Breathing)

Improves oxygen exchange, lowers blood pressure, and strengthens the respiratory system. It's great for reducing anxiety and promoting deep relaxation.

  • Place one hand on your chest and the other on your belly.
  • Inhale deeply through your nose, expanding your belly.
  • Exhale slowly through your nose, feeling your belly deflate.
  • Continue for 20 breaths, focusing on deep, slow breathing.

Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana)

Balances energy, calms the nervous system, and enhances mental clarity. It's commonly used in yoga and meditation practices.

  • Use your right thumb to close your right nostril.
  • Inhale through your left nostril, hold for 4 seconds and exhale.
  • Repeat with you right nostril.
  • Repeat for 5 minutes.

Pursed-Lip Breathing

Helps control breath, reduces shortness of breath, and improves lung function. It's particularly useful for people with respiratory conditions.

  • Inhale through your nose for 2 seconds.
  • Exhale slowly through pursed lips (as if blowing out a candle)
  • Hold your breath for 4 seconds.
  • Repeat for several minutes.

Circular Breathing

Supports continuous airflow, enhances endurance, and is often used in meditation and musical performance. It can help maintain a steady rhythm and focus.

  • Inhale deeply through your nose.
  • Exhale continuously while simultaneously inhaling through your nose.
  • Maintain a steady airflow without pauses

Resonant Breathing (Coherent Breathing)

Helps regulate the nervous system, reduces stress, and promotes relaxation. It can also improve heart rate variability, supporting cardiovascular health.

  • Inhale slowly through your nose for 6 seconds.
  • Exhale gently through your nose for 6 seconds..
  • Maintain this steady rhythm for 5-10 minutes.

Sitali Breath (Cooling Breath)

Lowers body temperature, reduces stress and anxiety, and enhances mental clarity. Studies suggest it may increase alpha, delta, and theta brain wave activity, promoting a calm state.

  • Curl your tongue into a tube (or purse your lips if you can't curl your tongue).
  • Inhale deeply through the curled tongue or pursed lips
  • Close your mouth and exhale slowly through your nose.

Humming Bee Breath (Bhramari)

Soothes the nervous system, enhances focus, and relieves anxiety. It stimulates the Ajna Chakra (Third Eye), improving concentration and emotional balance.

  • Sit in a quiet space and close your eyes.
  • Inhale deeply through your nose.
  • Exhale while making a low humming sound, like a bee.
  • Repeat for 5-10 rounds, focusing on the vibration.
